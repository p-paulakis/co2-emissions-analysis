# CO2 Emissions Analysis

## Overview
Global CO2 emissions analysis covering 215 countries from 1750 to 2024.

## Key Findings
I analysed a global CO2 emissions dataset from Our World in Data, covering 
215 countries from 1750 to 2024. During cleaning, rows with missing CO2 
values were removed, leaving 23,408 records for analysis. China is the 
largest total emitter (12,172 million tonnes in 2023), followed by the USA 
and India — however on a per capita basis, Qatar leads globally at 40 tonnes 
per person, highlighting that small oil-rich nations have a disproportionate 
carbon footprint. Global emissions remained relatively stable until the 1950s, 
when post-war industrialisation triggered a dramatic acceleration. Despite a 
brief dip during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, emissions recovered sharply — 
suggesting that structural changes required by the Paris Agreement are not yet 
materialising.
